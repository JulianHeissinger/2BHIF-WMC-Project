History of Porsche

Porsche was founded in 1931 by Ferdinand Porsche in Stuttgart, Germany, initially as a vehicle development consultancy. The brand became renowned for creating the Volkswagen Beetle, one of the most iconic cars of all time. In 1948, Porsche introduced its first sports car, the 356, which laid the foundation for its reputation in high-performance engineering. The legendary Porsche 911, introduced in 1964, became the brand's hallmark, known for its distinctive design and exceptional handling. Today, Porsche remains a leader in the luxury sports car market, evolving with electric models like the Taycan.
