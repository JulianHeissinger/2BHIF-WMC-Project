History of the Automobile

The automobile was born in the late 19th century, with Carl Benz's creation of the first gasoline-powered car in 1885.

Early models were slow, expensive, and not widely available, but innovations soon followed.

Henry Ford revolutionized the industry with mass production in the early 20th century, making cars affordable to many.

Henry Ford beside his car

The introduction of electric starters, improved engines, and road infrastructure fueled the car's popularity.

By the mid-20th century, cars became a symbol of freedom, convenience, and modern living.

Today, the automobile continues to evolve with electric and autonomous technologies shaping the future.
